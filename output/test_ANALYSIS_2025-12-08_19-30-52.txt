3) RESEARCH QUESTIONS

1. Under this compound-stressor scenario, how does the posterior probability of a > 48 h blackout evolve after observing:
    - simultaneous hurricane damage,
    - confirmed cyber wiper activity, and
    - cloud-control loss?
2. How sensitive is the posterior risk of transformer destruction to:
    - ambient temperature excursions above 112 °F, and
    - overload magnitude/duration, using Arrhenius-type thermal aging?
3. Does repeated Bayesian updating across multiple extreme observations converge to stable posteriors for:
    - blackout > 48 h,
    - > 40 GW load loss, and
    - nuclear SCRAM/no-restart,
   or do the posteriors saturate near 1.0 under this stress model?
4. Where does the first-order PRA approximation (independent events, single-fault focus) clearly break down when we compare:
    - PRA results, vs.
    - Monte Carlo simulations using correlated failures and common-cause shocks?
5. What additional structure (explicit fault trees, common-cause groups, cascade models) yields the largest reduction in model uncertainty for this scenario per unit of model complexity?
6. How does staffing level (40% vs 70% vs 90% available) change:
    - time-to-restoration,
    - probability of mis-operations,
   when fed back into the PRA and reliability layers?
7. What is the expected remaining life lost (in equivalent operating hours) for transformers subjected to:
    - 72 hours at 112 °F with 120% load,
   vs baseline 25 °C operation at rated load?
8. How much does adding an independent on-prem EMS instance reduce:
    - the PRA-estimated risk of blackout > 48 h,
   conditional on the same hurricane + cyber event?
