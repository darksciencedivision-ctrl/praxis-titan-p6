2) RISK ANALYSIS

| Domain | Risk | Mechanism | Consequence | Possible Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Technical / Engineering | Cybersecurity breaches | Exploits of vulnerabilities in hardware or software | System disruptions, power outages, potential fatalities from uncontrolled situations | Regular security audits, software updates, hardware fortification |
| Social / Governance | Inadequate emergency response planning | Lack of coordination among local and federal agencies | Delayed recovery times, increased property damage, potential loss of life | Establishing clear lines of communication and roles for various agencies during emergencies |
| Operational / Process | Equipment failures or malfunctions | Aging infrastructure, insufficient maintenance | System disruptions, power outages, potential fatalities from uncontrolled situations | Regular equipment inspections and preventative maintenance programs |

3) RESEARCH QUESTIONS

1. What are the most common types of vulnerabilities in modern grid components, and how can they be mitigated?
2. How effective are current cybersecurity measures against coordinated cyber-physical attacks on critical infrastructure?
3. Can machine learning algorithms effectively detect and respond to emerging threats in real-time?
4. What is the optimal balance between decentralizing control and maintaining centralized oversight for a national power grid?
5. How can emergency response planning be improved among various agencies to minimize the impact of large-scale power outages?
6. Which technological advancements could enhance the resilience of the power grid against cyber-physical attacks?
7. Can AI supervisors effectively maintain system stability during periods of high demand and potential threats?
8. What are the most effective methods for detecting and responding to insider threats within critical infrastructure systems?
9. How can we better understand the human factors that contribute to successful or unsuccessful cyber-physical attacks on power grids?
10. Are there any significant trade-offs between privacy concerns and cybersecurity measures in a national power grid?
