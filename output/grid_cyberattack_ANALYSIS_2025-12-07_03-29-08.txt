2) RISK ANALYSIS

Domain  | Risk  | Mechanism  | Consequence  | Possible Mitigation
--- | --- | --- | --- | ---
Technical / Engineering  | Cybersecurity breaches in the grid's digital infrastructure. | Malware infections, unauthorized access to control systems. | Cascading blackouts, widespread power outages. | Regular security audits, software updates, and employee training on cybersecurity best practices.
Social / Governance  | Inadequate regulatory frameworks or lack of public awareness about cyber threats. | Insufficient legal or policy measures to address emerging risks. | Limited ability to respond effectively to attacks. | Strengthening regulatory frameworks, increasing public education on the importance of cybersecurity in critical infrastructure systems.
Operational / Process  | Inefficient emergency response procedures or inadequate backup power sources. | Poorly defined roles and responsibilities during crisis situations. | Prolonged outages and delayed recovery efforts. | Developing clear emergency response plans, ensuring adequate backup power generation capacity for critical facilities.

3) RESEARCH QUESTIONS

1. What are the most effective machine learning algorithms for detecting anomalous behavior in power grid data?
2. How can superconducting materials improve the efficiency of voltage conversion in substations?
3. Can blockchain technology enhance cybersecurity and integrity in the management of a national power grid?
4. Which regulatory frameworks are best suited to address emerging risks in cyber-physical attacks on critical infrastructure systems?
5. What is the optimal mix of renewable energy sources that can ensure grid stability during peak demand periods?
6. How do different types of transmission lines (overhead or underground) impact energy efficiency and vulnerability to physical damage?
7. Can advanced robotics and automation improve the speed and effectiveness of power grid maintenance and recovery efforts after a cyber-physical attack?
8. What are the most effective methods for detecting and mitigating coordinated attacks on multiple power grid components simultaneously?
9. How can AI supervisors enhance grid stability during periods of high demand or potential threats?
10. What are the long-term consequences of widespread adoption of smart grid technologies on energy efficiency, cost, and environmental impact?
